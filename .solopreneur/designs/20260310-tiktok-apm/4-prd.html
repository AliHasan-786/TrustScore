Product Requirements Document (PRD)

TrustScore-RAG: Multi-Agent Moderation Triage

1. Objective & Problem Statement

As TikTok contends with rapidly evolving safety threats (FIMI networks, AIGC misinformation), traditional classification models are inadequate. Relying entirely on unconstrained LLMs causes harmful hallucinations in deeply nuanced policy areas (e.g., civic integrity vs satire). Passing all borderline text to human operators breaks Operational Expenditure (OpEx) budgets and causes massive latency, letting viral harm escape. Finally, human mediators suffer from automation bias, blindly trusting AI scores over 93%.

Solution: A cost-aware, multi-agent framework utilizing RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) and an LLM Performance Predictor (LPP) to surgically route clear actions while surfacing highly confused, epistemic anomalies back to humans via an Explainable AI (XAI) dashboard.

2. Architecture & TTFT Trade-offs

  • Retrieval Agent: Embeddings matched against an internal policy KB + authorized external endpoints (WHO, EU DSA).
  • Latency (TTFT) Considerations: Agentic orchestration adds ~1.5–3.0s latency. For synchronous tasks like Livestream moderation, this is unacceptable. Therefore, TrustScore-RAG only acts as a Triage layer (Layer 2) for items that bypass standard deterministic keyword filters (Layer 1).
